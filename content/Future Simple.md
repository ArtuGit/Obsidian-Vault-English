The Future Simple (with *will*) expresses predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises, offers, and facts about the future. It's the most common way to talk about the future in English. Compare with [[Going to Future]] for plans and intentions.

---

## Form

### ✅ Positive (Affirmative)

| Subject | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| All subjects | will + base verb | I **will call** you later. |

**Contraction:** will → **'ll**
- I will → I'll, you will → you'll, he will → he'll, etc.

### ❌ Negative

| Subject | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| All subjects | will not (won't) + base verb | She **won't come** to the party. |

### ❓ Questions

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Yes/No | Will + subject + base verb? | **Will** you **help** me? |
| Wh- | Wh-word + will + subject + base verb? | **When will** you **arrive**? |

### Short Answers

| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Will you come? | Yes, I **will**. | No, I **won't**. |
| Will she help? | Yes, she **will**. | No, she **won't**. |

## Uses

### 1. Predictions (Opinions About the Future)
Things you think or believe will happen (without evidence).

- I think it **will rain** tomorrow.
- She **will probably** be late.
- They **won't win** the match.

### 2. Spontaneous Decisions
Decisions made at the moment of speaking.

- I'm hungry. I **will order** a pizza.
- The phone is ringing. I**'ll get** it.
- I don't have cash. I**'ll pay** by card.

### 3. Promises
Committing to do something in the future.

- I **will always love** you.
- I **won't tell** anyone your secret.
- I **will pay** you back next week.

### 4. Offers and Requests
Offering to help or asking someone to do something.

- **Will** you **help** me with this? *(request)*
- I**'ll carry** that for you. *(offer)*
- **Will** you **marry** me? *(request)*
- I**'ll make** you some coffee. *(offer)*

### 5. Threats and Warnings
Expressing negative consequences.

- If you don't study, you **will fail**.
- Be careful, or you**'ll hurt** yourself.
- I**'ll call** the police if you don't leave.

### 6. Future Facts and Certainties
Things that are sure to happen.

- The sun **will rise** at 6:30 AM tomorrow.
- She **will be** 30 next year.
- The meeting **will start** at 9 AM.

### 7. First Conditional
Real/possible future situations.

- If it rains, I **will stay** home.
- If you study, you **will pass**.
- I**'ll help** you if you need me.

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| I will to call you. | I **will call** you. | No *to* after *will* |
| She will comes later. | She **will come** later. | Base verb after *will* (no -s) |
| I will going to help. | I **will help** OR I **am going to** help. | Don't mix *will* and *going to* |
| Will you to help me? | **Will** you **help** me? | No *to* after *will* |
| I think it will to rain. | I think it **will rain**. | Base verb after *will* |
| I go to the store later. | I **will go** to the store later. | Need *will* for future |

## Shall (British English / Formal)

"Shall" can replace "will" with I/we in offers and suggestions:

| Usage | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Offers | **Shall** I open the window? |
| Suggestions | **Shall** we go out for dinner? |
| Asking for advice | What **shall** I do? |

**Note:** In modern English, especially American English, *will* is more common.

## Tense Comparison: Will vs. Going to

| Will (Future Simple) | Going to |
|---------------------|----------|
| Spontaneous decisions | Planned decisions |
| Predictions (opinion) | Predictions (evidence) |
| Promises, offers, requests | Intentions |
| Decided NOW | Decided BEFORE |

**Examples:**

| Will | Going to |
|------|----------|
| I**'ll have** the pasta. *(deciding now in restaurant)* | I**'m going to have** the pasta. *(already decided)* |
| I think it **will rain**. *(my opinion)* | Look at those clouds—it**'s going to rain**. *(evidence)* |
| I**'ll help** you. *(offer)* | I**'m going to help** her tomorrow. *(plan)* |
| Don't worry, I **won't forget**. *(promise)* | I**'m going to call** him later. *(intention)* |

---

## Will vs. Present Continuous (for Future)

| Will | Present Continuous |
|------|-------------------|
| Predictions, decisions | Arranged plans |
| Not specifically planned | Specific arrangements made |
| I'll see you soon. *(general)* | I'm seeing John at 3. *(arranged, in my calendar)* |

---

## Will vs. Present Simple (for Future)

| Will | Present Simple |
|------|----------------|
| Predictions, decisions | Timetables, schedules |
| Not fixed | Fixed, official |
| I'll arrive around 5. *(estimate)* | The train arrives at 5. *(scheduled)* |

## Passive Voice

The Future Simple Passive is commonly used for predictions, announcements, and formal statements about what will happen.

### Form

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Positive** | will be + past participle | The package **will be delivered** tomorrow. |
| **Negative** | will not (won't) be + past participle | The meeting **won't be cancelled**. |
| **Question** | Will + subject + be + past participle? | **Will** the project **be finished** on time? |

### Examples

**Predictions and certainties:**
- The results **will be announced** next week.
- The winner **will be chosen** by a panel of judges.
- The new law **will be implemented** in January.

**Promises and assurances:**
- Your order **will be shipped** within 24 hours.
- The problem **will be fixed** immediately.
- You **will be informed** as soon as we know.

**Formal announcements:**
- The ceremony **will be held** at the town hall.
- Refreshments **will be served** after the presentation.
- All participants **will be notified** by email.

**First conditional:**
- If the weather is bad, the event **will be postponed**.
- The refund **will be processed** if you return the item.

## Practice Tips

1. **Spontaneous reactions:** Practice making instant decisions. "Someone's at the door—I'll get it. I'm thirsty—I'll have some water."

2. **Prediction game:** Make predictions about the future. "I think AI will change education. I don't think people will stop using cars."

3. **Offers and promises:** Practice offering help and making promises. "I'll help you with your homework. I won't be late tomorrow."
