**Conditionals** are sentences that describe a condition and its possible result. They usually have an **"if" clause** (the condition) and a **main clause** (the result).

---

## 1. **Zero Conditional** – General truths and facts

**Form**: If + present simple, present simple  
**Example**: If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

## 2. **First Conditional** – Real future possibilities

**Form**: If + present simple, will + base verb  
**Example**: If it rains tomorrow, we’ll cancel the picnic.

## 3. **Second Conditional** – Unreal or unlikely present/future

**Form**: If + past simple, would + base verb  
**Example**: If I had a car, I would drive to work.

## 4. **Third Conditional** – Unreal past (regret or hypothetical)

**Form**: If + past perfect, would have + past participle  
**Example**: If she had studied, she would have passed the exam.

## 5. **Mixed Conditionals** – Mixed time references

**Examples**:

- If I had studied (past), I would be successful now (present).
    
- If I were you (present), I would have helped him (past).
