The indefinite article (**a/an**) usually means **“one of many”** or **“not identified”**.

British English is the default in this vault. When American usage differs, see “American version”.

---

## Form

### ✅ a vs an (sound rule)
Use **an** before a **vowel sound**, not before a vowel letter.

| Word | Article | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **an** apple | an | vowel sound /æ/ |
| **a** banana | a | consonant sound /b/ |
| **an** hour | an | silent “h” → vowel sound /aʊ/ |
| **a** university | a | starts with /juː/ (“y” sound) |
| **an** MBA | an | starts with /em/ |

---

## Main uses (B1/B2 core)

### 1) First mention
- I saw **a** dog in the park.

### 2) “One of many” / not specific
- Can you recommend **a** good film?

### 3) Classification (jobs, roles, types)
- She’s **a** teacher.
- It’s **an** electric car.

### 4) A new example of a category
- That’s **a** very interesting idea.

---

## When you usually DON’T use a/an

### Uncountable nouns
- Ø information, Ø advice, Ø furniture
✅ I need Ø information. *(not: an information)*

### Plural nouns
- Ø books, Ø ideas
✅ I need Ø books. *(not: a books)*

### When another determiner is used
- my / this / each / every / some
✅ I met **my** friend. *(not: a my friend)*

---

## Common patterns

### a/an + adjective + noun
- It’s **a** good day.
- She has **an** interesting job.

### a/an with meaning “per”
- twice **a** week
- £10 **a** kilo
- 60 miles **an** hour
See [[Measurement and Frequency Expressions (a-an per)]].

---

## Common mistakes (and fixes)

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---|---|---|
| She is Ø doctor. | She is **a** doctor. | Job/classification |
| I bought Ø umbrella. | I bought **an** umbrella. | Singular count noun |
| It’s **an** university. | It’s **a** university. | /juː/ sound |
| I need **a** information. | I need Ø information. | Uncountable |

## Practice

### A) Choose a/an or Ø
1) He bought ___ old car.
2) I need ___ advice.
3) She’s ___ honest person.
4) They have ___ children.
5) It’s ___ one-hour lesson.
6) I’m looking for ___ job.

### B) Fix the mistakes
7) I saw an university near here.
8) She wants a furniture for her room.
9) He is engineer.
10) Can I have a water?

---

## Answer key

1) an  
2) Ø  
3) an  
4) Ø  
5) a *(“one-hour” starts with /w/ sound)*  
6) a  

7) I saw **a** university near here.  
8) She wants Ø furniture for her room. *(or: a piece of furniture)*  
9) He is **an** engineer.  
10) Can I have Ø water? *(or: a glass/bottle of water)*

## C1+ Notes

### “A certain” and speaker attitude
- I met **a certain** Mr Smith. *(the speaker assumes you may know the name or wants to sound formal/mysterious)*

### “Such a / quite a / what a”
- She’s **such a** good friend.
- It was **quite a** surprise.
- **What a** mess!

See [[Quantifier Expressions that Look Like Articles]].
