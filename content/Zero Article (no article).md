“Zero article” means **no article** (Ø). It’s one of the hardest parts for learners because English uses Ø a lot.

British English is the default in this vault. When American usage differs, see “American version”.

---

## Core uses (B1/B2)

### 1) Plural generic
Talk about a whole category.
- Ø Dogs are loyal.
- Ø Smartphones are expensive.

### 2) Uncountable generic
- Ø Water is essential.
- Ø Information is important.

### 3) Most proper names
- Ø London, Ø Mary, Ø Heathrow, Ø Mount Everest
But some need **the** (see [[Proper Nouns and Place Names (Articles)]])

### 4) Meals, days, languages (common patterns)
- I have Ø breakfast.
- See you on Ø Monday.
- She speaks Ø French.

### 5) Fixed expressions
- by Ø car, on Ø foot, at Ø home
See also [[Institutions: School, Hospital, Work, Home]].

---

## Contrast examples

### General vs specific
- Ø Coffee is healthy for some people. *(coffee in general)*
- The coffee is cold. *(this coffee)*

### Generic plural vs specific plural
- Ø Students need practice. *(students in general)*
- The students in my class are great. *(a known group)*

## Common mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---|---|---|
| I like **the** nature. | I like Ø nature. | general idea |
| She speaks **the** English. | She speaks Ø English. | languages |
| We go by **the** car. | We go by Ø car. | fixed phrase |

## Practice

### A) Choose Ø or **the**
1) ___ life is complicated.
2) ___ life in this city is expensive.
3) I drink ___ tea every day. (generic)
4) ___ tea you made is delicious.
5) He goes to work by ___ bus.
6) ___ bus is late again. (the one we’re waiting for)

### B) Fix the mistakes
7) I like the history.
8) She studies the French.
9) We usually have the dinner at 7.

---

## Answer key

1) Ø  
2) the  
3) Ø  
4) the  
5) Ø  
6) the  

7) I like Ø history. *(or: I like **the** history of this country)*  
8) She studies Ø French.  
9) We usually have Ø dinner at 7. *(or: We had **the** dinner you cooked)*

## C1+ Notes

### Zero article is common in “abstract” talk
- Ø education, Ø happiness, Ø progress
But you can use **the** when you mean a specific context:
- The education I received was excellent.

### Headline style
Headlines often drop articles for brevity.
See [[Articles in Headlines and Titles]].

---

## American version
Mostly the same. Differences are mainly in institution phrases:
- BrE: at university / in hospital
- AmE: in college / in the hospital
