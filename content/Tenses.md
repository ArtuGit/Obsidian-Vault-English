**Tenses** indicate the time of an action or state expressed by a verb. English has three main time frames—**past**, **present**, and **future**—each with four aspects: simple, continuous, perfect, and perfect continuous.

---

## 1. **Present Tenses**

### [[Present Simple]]
**Form**: Subject + base verb (+ -s/-es for 3rd person singular)  
**Use**: Habits, routines, general truths, and permanent states  
**Example**: She works in a hospital.

### [[Present Continuous]]
**Form**: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing  
**Use**: Actions happening now, temporary situations, future arrangements  
**Example**: They are watching a movie right now.

### [[Present Perfect]]
**Form**: Subject + have/has + past participle  
**Use**: Past actions with present relevance, experiences, unfinished time periods  
**Example**: I have visited Paris three times.

### [[Present Perfect Continuous]]
**Form**: Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing  
**Use**: Actions that started in the past and continue, emphasis on duration  
**Example**: She has been studying for two hours.

---

## 2. **Past Tenses**

### [[Past Simple]]
**Form**: Subject + verb-ed (or irregular form)  
**Use**: Completed actions at a specific past time  
**Example**: We traveled to Japan last year.

### [[Past Continuous]]
**Form**: Subject + was/were + verb-ing  
**Use**: Actions in progress at a past moment, interrupted actions, background context  
**Example**: I was reading when the phone rang.

### [[Past Perfect]]
**Form**: Subject + had + past participle  
**Use**: Actions completed before another past action  
**Example**: She had already left when I arrived.

### [[Past Perfect Continuous]]
**Form**: Subject + had + been + verb-ing  
**Use**: Duration of an action before another past event  
**Example**: They had been waiting for an hour before the bus came.

---

## 3. **Future Tenses**

### [[Future Simple]]
**Form**: Subject + will + base verb  
**Use**: Predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises  
**Example**: It will rain tomorrow.

### [[Going to Future]]
**Form**: Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb  
**Use**: Plans, intentions, predictions based on evidence  
**Example**: I am going to start a new job next week.

### [[Future Continuous]]
**Form**: Subject + will + be + verb-ing  
**Use**: Actions in progress at a future time  
**Example**: This time tomorrow, I will be flying to London.

### [[Future Perfect]]
**Form**: Subject + will + have + past participle  
**Use**: Actions completed before a future point  
**Example**: By next month, she will have finished her degree.

### [[Future Perfect Continuous]]
**Form**: Subject + will + have + been + verb-ing  
**Use**: Duration of an action up to a future point  
**Example**: By 2026, I will have been working here for ten years.
