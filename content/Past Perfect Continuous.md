The Past Perfect Continuous describes an action that was in progress before another past event, emphasizing duration or the ongoing nature of the earlier activity. Compare with [[Past Perfect]] which focuses on completion.

---

## Form

### ✅ Positive (Affirmative)

| Subject | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| All subjects | had been + verb-**ing** | I **had been waiting** for an hour. |

### ❌ Negative

| Subject | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| All subjects | had not (hadn't) been + verb-ing | She **hadn't been sleeping** well. |

### ❓ Questions

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Yes/No | Had + subject + been + verb-ing? | **Had** you **been waiting** long? |
| Wh- | Wh-word + had + subject + been + verb-ing? | **How long had** you **been studying**? |

### Short Answers

| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Had you been working? | Yes, I **had**. | No, I **hadn't**. |
| Had they been running? | Yes, they **had**. | No, they **hadn't**. |

---

## Uses

### 1. Duration of an Activity Before a Past Event
Emphasizing how long something had been happening before another past action.

- I **had been waiting** for two hours when the bus finally came.
- She **had been studying** for months before the exam.
- They **had been living** there for ten years before they moved.

### 2. Recent Activity with Visible Past Results
An activity that had been happening and produced visible evidence at a past moment.

- He was tired because he **had been working** all day.
- Her eyes were red—she **had been crying**.
- The ground was wet because it **had been raining**.

### 3. Explaining Past Situations
Why things were the way they were at a moment in the past.

- I was exhausted because I **had been running**.
- She was angry because he **had been ignoring** her calls.
- My hands were dirty because I **had been gardening**.

### 4. Repeated Actions Over a Period Before a Past Time
Actions that happened repeatedly leading up to a past moment.

- She **had been calling** him all morning before he answered.
- I **had been trying** to find a job for months before I got one.
- He **had been visiting** her every day before she recovered.

### 5. Unfinished Actions Interrupted by a Past Event
Ongoing actions that were interrupted.

- I **had been reading** for an hour when the lights went out.
- We **had been discussing** the issue when the boss arrived.
- She **had been cooking** when the smoke alarm went off.

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| I had been work all day. | I **had been working** all day. | Need verb + -ing |
| She had being waiting. | She **had been** waiting. | *Been*, not *being* |
| I had been knowing him for years. | I **had known** him for years. | Stative verbs don't take continuous |
| We had been wait for hours. | We **had been waiting** for hours. | Need -ing form |
| Before the exam, I had been studied. | Before the exam, I **had been studying**. | Need -ing form |
| How long you had been waiting? | **How long had** you **been waiting**? | Invert *had* and subject |

## Tense Comparison: Past Perfect Continuous vs. Past Perfect

| Past Perfect Continuous | Past Perfect |
|------------------------|--------------|
| Emphasizes **duration/process** | Emphasizes **completion/result** |
| Focus on **how long** | Focus on **what/how many** |
| Activity may have been **incomplete** | Activity was **complete** |
| Had been + verb-ing | Had + past participle |

**Examples:**

| Past Perfect Continuous | Past Perfect |
|------------------------|--------------|
| I **had been reading** for an hour. *(focus on duration)* | I **had read** the book. *(focus on completion)* |
| She **had been writing** emails. *(process)* | She **had written** 10 emails. *(number/result)* |
| They **had been walking** all day. *(emphasizes tiredness)* | They **had walked** 20 km. *(emphasizes distance)* |
| He **had been learning** French. *(ongoing process)* | He **had learned** French. *(achievement)* |

---

## Past Perfect Continuous vs. Past Continuous

| Past Perfect Continuous | Past Continuous |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| Before another past event | At a past moment |
| Had been + verb-ing | Was/Were + verb-ing |
| Duration up to a past point | Action in progress at a past time |

**Examples:**
- I **had been working** for 3 hours before she arrived. *(duration before another past event)*
- I **was working** when she arrived. *(action in progress at that moment)*

**Timeline:**
```
[Past Perfect Continuous] -----> [Past Simple] -----> [NOW]
   (ongoing before)              (reference point)
```

## Stative Verbs (No Continuous Form)

Use [[Past Perfect]] instead for these verbs:

| Category | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Mental states | I **had known** him for years. *(not: had been knowing)* |
| Emotions | She **had loved** him since childhood. |
| Possession | They **had had** the house for decades. |
| Senses | He **had always believed** in her. |

---

## When to Use Past Perfect Continuous

### ✅ Use when:
- You want to emphasize how long an activity had been going on
- The activity caused a visible result at a past moment
- Explaining past tiredness, emotions, or conditions
- The activity was ongoing or unfinished before another past event

### ❌ Don't use when:
- The focus is on completion or the number of things done
- Using stative verbs
- The duration isn't important

**Duration important:**
- I **had been working** all day, so I was exhausted. *(emphasizes the long process)*

**Completion important:**
- I **had finished** my work, so I was free. *(emphasizes the result)*

## Passive Voice

⚠️ **The Past Perfect Continuous Passive is grammatically possible but extremely rare.** It's awkward and almost never used in practice.

### Form

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Positive** | had been being + past participle | The building **had been being renovated**. |

### Why It's Rarely Used

The structure "had been being" is considered very clumsy. Native speakers avoid it in favor of simpler alternatives:

| Instead of (awkward) | Use (natural) |
|---------------------|---------------|
| The road **had been being repaired** for months. | The road **had been under repair** for months. |
| The case **had been being investigated**. | The case **had been investigated** for months. *(Past Perfect Passive)* |
| The project **had been being developed**. | They **had been developing** the project. *(Active voice)* |

**Recommendation:** Avoid this construction entirely. Use [[Past Perfect]] Passive or rewrite in active voice.

## Practice Tips

1. **Explain past states:** Think of past situations and explain them. "I was tired because I had been exercising. I was hungry because I hadn't been eating properly."

2. **How long before...:** Practice duration questions. "How long had you been learning English before you moved abroad? I had been studying for 5 years."

3. **Story background:** Set scenes in stories. "The detective was tired. He had been working on the case for weeks. He had been interviewing witnesses all day."
