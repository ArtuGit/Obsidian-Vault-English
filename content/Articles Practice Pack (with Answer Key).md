This is a mixed practice pack for **all article patterns**:
- **a/an** (indefinite)
- **the** (definite)
- **Ø** (zero article)
- generic reference
- institution phrases (school/hospital)
- place names (the UK, the Alps, Ø France)

British English is the default in this vault. When American usage differs, see “American version”.

---

## Set 1 — Choose a/an, the, or Ø (20 items)

5) ___ history of this building is fascinating.

9) I need ___ information about visas.
10) ___ information you gave me was helpful.
11) We went to ___ cinema on Saturday.
12) I usually have Ø breakfast at 7.
13) She speaks Ø English very well.
14) ___ English you used in that email was too informal.

16) He lives in Ø Italy.
17) She lives in ___ Netherlands.
18) Our flight leaves from Ø Heathrow.
19) I’m at ___ airport now.
20) It costs £10 ___ kilo.

## Set 2 — Institution meaning (Ø vs the)

Choose the best option.

21) He’s in (Ø / the) hospital after the accident. *(BrE patient meaning)*
22) I’m going to (Ø / the) hospital to visit my friend.
23) She goes to (Ø / the) school every day.
24) I went to (Ø / the) school to talk to the headteacher.
25) He’s at (Ø / the) work right now.
26) I left my phone at (Ø / the) home.

33) I like Ø music.  
34) We go by Ø car.  
35) He speaks Ø English.

37) This is **the** first time here.

40) We visited **the** Alps last year.  
41) He lives in Ø France.  
42) I’m at Ø Heathrow Airport, and I’m at Ø Heathrow now. *(name)* / I’m at the airport now. *(place)*

Yesterday I went to **the** cinema with **a** friend. We watched **a** film about **a** detective. **The** film was interesting, but **the** ending was strange. After **the** movie, we had Ø dinner in **a** restaurant near **the** station. I like Ø food there.
