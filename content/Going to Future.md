The "Going to" Future expresses plans, intentions, and predictions based on present evidence. It emphasizes that a decision was made before the moment of speaking. Compare with [[Future Simple]] for spontaneous decisions and opinion-based predictions.

---

## Form

### ✅ Positive (Affirmative)

| Subject | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| I | am going to + base verb | I **am going to travel** next month. |
| He / She / It | is going to + base verb | She **is going to start** a new job. |
| You / We / They | are going to + base verb | They **are going to move** to Spain. |

**Contractions:** I'm going to, he's going to, we're going to, etc.
**Informal speech:** "going to" → "gonna" (pronunciation only, not written)

### ❌ Negative

| Subject | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| I | am not going to | I**'m not going to** accept that offer. |
| He / She / It | is not (isn't) going to | He **isn't going to** come. |
| You / We / They | are not (aren't) going to | We **aren't going to** wait any longer. |

### ❓ Questions

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Yes/No | Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + verb? | **Are** you **going to** help? |
| Wh- | Wh-word + am/is/are + subject + going to? | **What are** you **going to** do? |

### Short Answers

| Question | Yes | No |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Are you going to come? | Yes, I **am**. | No, I**'m not**. |
| Is she going to call? | Yes, she **is**. | No, she **isn't**. |

## Uses

### 1. Plans and Intentions
Decisions made before the moment of speaking.

- I **am going to study** medicine after school.
- She **is going to buy** a new car next month.
- We **are going to visit** our grandparents this weekend.

### 2. Predictions Based on Present Evidence
When you can see evidence that something will happen.

- Look at those clouds! It **is going to rain**.
- She's very pale. I think she **is going to faint**.
- The car is speeding. It **is going to crash**!

### 3. Inevitable or Certain Events
Things that are clearly going to happen.

- She **is going to have** a baby in June.
- The company **is going to close** next year.
- He **is going to be** 50 next week.

### 4. Expressing Determination
Strong intention or resolve to do something.

- I **am going to pass** this exam no matter what!
- She **is going to succeed**—nothing will stop her.
- I **am not going to** let them win.

### 5. Scheduled Personal Plans
Plans that you have arranged for the future.

- I **am going to meet** my friend after work.
- They **are going to have** a party on Saturday.
- She **is going to see** the doctor tomorrow.

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| I going to study. | I **am going to** study. | Need *am/is/are* |
| She is going study. | She **is going to** study. | Need *to* |
| I am go to travel. | I **am going to** travel. | Need full structure |
| Are you go to help? | **Are** you **going to** help? | Need full structure |
| He is going to to come. | He **is going to** come. | Only one *to* |
| They're going to went. | They**'re going to go**. | Base verb after *to* |
| I will going to help. | I **am going to** help. OR I **will** help. | Don't mix structures |

## Tense Comparison: Going to vs. Will

| Going to | Will |
|----------|------|
| Pre-planned decisions | Spontaneous decisions |
| Evidence-based predictions | Opinion-based predictions |
| Intentions | Promises, offers |
| Decided BEFORE now | Decided NOW |

**Examples:**

| Going to | Will |
|----------|------|
| I**'m going to buy** a new phone. *(I've decided, I've done research)* | I**'ll buy** you a coffee. *(offer, decided now)* |
| She**'s going to fail**—she hasn't studied. *(evidence)* | I think she **will fail**. *(my opinion)* |
| We**'re going to get** married next year. *(plan)* | I **will always love** you. *(promise)* |
| Look! He**'s going to fall**! *(I can see it happening)* | I think it **will be** sunny tomorrow. *(prediction)* |

---

## Going to vs. Present Continuous (for Future)

Both can describe future plans, but:

| Going to | Present Continuous |
|----------|-------------------|
| Intentions and plans | Fixed arrangements |
| Decision made | Arrangements made with others |
| No specific details needed | Often has specific time/place |

**Examples:**
- I**'m going to see** a movie this weekend. *(general plan)*
- I**'m seeing** John at 3 PM tomorrow. *(specific arrangement)*

**Both acceptable:**
- I'm going to meet her at 6. *(emphasizes intention)*
- I'm meeting her at 6. *(emphasizes arrangement)*

## Common Expressions with "Going to"

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| going to be + adjective | future state | It**'s going to be** hot tomorrow. |
| going to have to | future necessity | I**'m going to have to** leave early. |
| going to be able to | future ability | She**'s going to be able to** come. |
| not going to | refusing | I**'m not going to** do that. |

## "Gonna" in Spoken English

In casual speech, "going to" is often pronounced "gonna":

- I'm **gonna** call you later.
- She's **gonna** be angry.
- We're **gonna** have fun!

**Important:** 
- "Gonna" is pronunciation only—don't write it in formal contexts
- "Gonna" is NOT used when "going to" means traveling: "I'm going to Paris." *(not "gonna")*

## Passive Voice

The "Going to" Passive is used for planned actions or predictions based on evidence, when the focus is on what will happen rather than who will do it.

### Form

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Positive** | am/is/are + going to be + past participle | The house **is going to be sold**. |
| **Negative** | am/is/are + not + going to be + past participle | The project **isn't going to be finished** on time. |
| **Question** | Am/Is/Are + subject + going to be + past participle? | **Is** the road **going to be repaired**? |

### Examples

**Planned actions:**
- The building **is going to be demolished** next month.
- New software **is going to be installed** this week.
- The report **is going to be submitted** tomorrow.

**Predictions based on evidence:**
- Look at those clouds—the game **is going to be cancelled**.
- The bridge **is going to be closed**—there's too much damage.
- This product **is going to be discontinued**—sales are very low.

**Intentions (formal):**
- Changes **are going to be made** to the policy.
- All employees **are going to be informed** of the decision.
- The issue **is going to be addressed** at the next meeting.

## Practice Tips

1. **Weekend plans:** Describe your upcoming plans. "This weekend, I'm going to clean my apartment. I'm going to meet my friends. We're going to watch a movie."

2. **Predictions from evidence:** Look at situations and predict. "Look at the time—I'm going to be late! The sky is dark—it's going to rain."

3. **Life goals:** Talk about your intentions. "I'm going to learn a new language. I'm going to travel more. I'm not going to waste my time."
